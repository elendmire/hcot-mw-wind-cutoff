Threshold Sensitivity Analysis — Summary
=========================================
Baseline thresholds: θ_high=50.0 MW, θ_low=10.0 MW, θ_drop=80.0%
Baseline event count: 259

Grid: ±20% variation in 5 steps per threshold (125 combinations total)
  θ_high range: 40.0–60.0 MW
  θ_low  range: 8.0–12.0 MW
  θ_drop range: 64.0–96.0 %

Results:
  Minimum events across grid: 77  (-70.3% vs baseline)
  Maximum events across grid: 1021  (+294.2% vs baseline)

Paper text (Supplementary S2):
The detection algorithm was evaluated over a ±20% variation in each of the three
threshold parameters (θ_high, θ_low, θ_drop), yielding 125 threshold
combinations. The baseline configuration (θ_high = 50.0 MW, θ_low = 10.0 MW,
θ_drop = 80.0%) identifies 259 events. Across the full grid, the event
count ranges from 77 to 1021 events, a span of -70.3% to
+294.2% relative to the baseline. The heatmap (Figure S2) shows that the
result is most sensitive to θ_high: raising this threshold from 40 MW to 60 MW
consistently reduces event counts by filtering out lower-capacity cut-offs.
θ_low and θ_drop have smaller and more symmetric effects. These results confirm
that the qualitative conclusions of the study are robust to reasonable variations
in detection criteria.
